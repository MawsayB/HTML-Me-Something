There are many places, common and rare, that we might think we want to visit. While hotels are easy to book, there are many other destinations where I have laid my head or I have played. These places have been such a treasure to explore that I want to make sure that others are aware of some worthwhile experiences to have during your lifetime.

"If you foolishly ignore beauty, you will soon find yourself without it. Your life will be impoverished. But if you invest in beauty, it will remain with you all the days of your life."

-Frank Lloyd Wright


My Top 5 Architectural Experiences

  1. Lilly's Tiny Circus Home

    2. Location: St. Louis, Missouri


    Reasons to Visit:

    • Feels like an escape in the secluded backyard of another home and tucked behind a fence.
    • It's 130 square feet!
    • Walking distance from Tower Grove Park, Missouri Botanical Garden and unique restaurants.
    • Your toliet is a human litter box.

  2. Futuro Home

    3. Location: Woodruff, Wisconsin


    Reasons to Visit:

    • It's out of this world.
    • Located on a private lake and you get access to a row boat, paddle boat, and canoe.
    • In just a 15 minute drive to Minocqua, Wisconsin you can see the Min-Aqua Bats Water Ski Show.
    • The lake is loaded with fish. And you might even see some loons and a bald eagle.

  3. City Museum

    4. Location: St. Louis, Missouri


    Reasons to Visit:

    • You can climb, slide down, and touch every piece of art in this living, interactive art experience!
    • There is a circus show a few times a day that is FREE to see.
    • Slide down a 10-story slide that used to carry shoes.
    • Find the world's largest pencil and sit on it.

  4. The Arnold Jackson House

    5. Location: Beaver Dam, Wisconsin


    Reasons to Visit:

    • You can say you've stayed in a home that was once cut into three pieces and moved across the state.
    • You can sleep in the home of Frank Lloyd Wright's personal physician.
    • The fireplace sometimes has bats that swoop in.
    • The whole house is trimmed in mahogany.

  5. The Frank Lloyd Wright House in Ebsworth Park

    6. Location: Kirkwood, Missouri


    Reasons to Visit:

    • You can find countless parallelograms.
    • If you can find the corner window, you can open a complete corner of the house.
    • The furniture was also designed by Frank Lloyd Wright to match the home design.
    • You can ponder why the second bathroom was never used.
